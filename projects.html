Research

Projects & themes

Active directions in the lab, aligned with ongoing grants and student theses. For full publication lists see Publications or the CV (PDF).

Voice as a biomarker for mental health

With clinical and social-work partners we collect voice data and build machine learning pipelines to study depression and related conditions from acoustic features. Current ISF work tests robustness: can proposed voice-based signals resist intentional manipulation (“faking”)? Related translation effort: Voxwell, exploring real-world deployment of voice technology.

Graph neural networks & combinatorial reasoning

We study how GNNs internalize classical algorithmic ideas (e.g., support in satisfiability) and how that informs Max-Clique, sparse PCA, and related problems—connecting deep models to handcrafted combinatorial structure.

High-dimensional data & feature selection

We analyze when structure can be recovered in spiked models and sparse PCA, and we critically examine benchmark datasets for feature selection—many widely used sets are easier than theory-focused “hard” regimes suggest.

Semantic structure in social & mobility data

“Semantic dimension” analysis extends PCA-style thinking to interpretable axes in social platforms (e.g., activity vs. feedback). With large-scale mobility and socio-economic data we study how latent factors separate—and what that means for policy design.

NLP: stance, augmentation, and multimodal models

Unsupervised structural embeddings for stance detection; hate-speech data augmentation; multimodal stance with textual and structural cues—bridging geometry, graphs, and text.

Deliberation & multi-agent systems

Modeling Citizens’ Assemblies and related settings with formal criteria for deliberation quality, including open-mindedness and opinion dynamics on real assembly data.

Interdisciplinary collaborations

Projects span digital humanities and literary networks, psychological first aid and welfare-related AI, nuclear-instrument signal processing, poultry genotyping pipelines, and clinical voice studies (e.g., thyroid, diabetes)—always with a clear ML methodology core.
